# Key Findings

## 1. Weekly Subscribers Exhibit the Highest Churn Risk

Weekly subscribers recorded a churn rate of 66.7%, significantly higher than both Monthly and Annual subscribers (20%). This suggests that users with shorter subscription commitments are more likely to discontinue using the platform.

## 2. User Engagement Has a Strong Relationship with Retention

Users with session durations below 30 minutes exhibited a churn rate of 100%, while highly engaged users showed no observed churn. This indicates that engagement level is a strong predictor of user retention.

## 3. Long-Term Users Tend to Be More Retained

Only 1 out of 6 users who joined before 2023 churned. This suggests that users who remain active for longer periods are less likely to leave the platform.

## 4. Daily Streaks Show Strong Retention Characteristics

None of the users who adopted the Daily Streak feature churned. Among all analyzed features, Daily Streaks demonstrated one of the strongest associations with user retention.

## 5. User Satisfaction Appears Closely Linked to Retention

No users with ratings above 4.5 churned, while users with ratings below 3 exhibited significantly higher churn levels. This suggests that customer satisfaction is strongly related to retention.

## 6. Feature Adoption May Reduce Churn

Users who engaged with multiple platform features exhibited no observed churn in the dataset. This indicates that broader feature adoption may contribute to improved retention.

## 7. Annual Subscribers Show Higher Engagement

Annual subscribers recorded both the highest average ratings and the longest average session durations. This suggests that long-term subscribers are generally more engaged and satisfied with the platform.

## 8. Premium Users Demonstrate Deeper Product Engagement

Most Premium subscribers interacted with multiple platform features, indicating stronger product adoption compared to other user segments.
